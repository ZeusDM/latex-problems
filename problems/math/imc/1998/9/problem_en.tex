Given $ 0< c< 1$, we define $f(x) =
 \begin{cases}
 \frac{x}{c} & x  \in [0,c] \\
 \frac{1-x}{1-c} & x \in [c, 1]
\end{cases}   
$
Let $f^{n}(x)=f(f(...f(x)))$ . Show that for each positive integer $n$, $f^{n}$ has a non-zero finite nunber of fixed points which aren't fixed points of $f^k$ for $k<   n$.
