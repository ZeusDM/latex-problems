Let 
$c \ge 1$
 be an integer. Define a sequence of positive integers by 
$a_1 = c$
 and 
\[a_{n+1}=a_n^3-4c\cdot a_n^2+5c^2\cdot a_n+c\]
 for all 
$n\ge 1$.
 Prove that for each integer 
$n \ge 2$
 there exists a prime number 
$p$
 dividing 
$a_n$
 but none of the numbers 
$a_1 , \ldots , a_{n -1}$.
